## **Paylines and Ways to Win Explained**

The number of paylines may be different in most slots, but how they work remain the same.

Paylines are the lines across the reels where matching symbols need to land in order for you to win. Classic fruit machine style bull rush slots will also have fixed paylines that might be 10, 20, or 25, while contemporary versions may use alternative payout systems.

Most recent bull rush slots employ “ways to win” instead of traditional paylines. In these games, symbols on adjacent reels that match are treated as a win, irrespective of their position on the reel. This process multiplies the number of potential winning lines and fits well with a theme of plenty and prosperity.

Refer to the table below for a comparison between paylines and ways-to-win systems:

| System Type | How Wins Are Determined | Average Player Experience |
| --- | --- | --- |

| Adjustable Lines | Player selects number of active lines | Flexible betting control |

## **RTP Conundrums**

RTP, or Return to Player, represents the theoretical percentage of wagered money returned to players over time. Most bull rush slots fall within the 95–97 percent RTP range, similar to many other modern slots.

RTP is calculated across millions or billions of spins and does not predict individual outcomes. Short-term losses are possible even in high-RTP games, particularly those with higher variance. Awareness of RTP allows players to make more informed choices when selecting between similar games.
